Abstract

EmpaAva is an embodied empathetic 3D avatar system designed for real-time, video-call-like interaction between users and digital humans. Given microphone and optional camera input, the system performs speech transcription and emotion perception, constructs an empathy-aware dialogue state, plans an appropriate response, and synthesizes expressive speech for a selected avatar persona. The generated speech is further converted into lip-synchronized facial motion and rendered as a photorealistic 3D avatar response.

The project targets EMNLP/ACL-style demo scenarios where language, affect, speech, and embodied rendering are presented as a complete interactive pipeline. The interface supports avatar selection, background selection, conversation history, generated video playback, and inspection of intermediate runtime artifacts.

Method Overview

01

Multimodal Perception

The booth records speech and optional camera input, then extracts ASR results and acoustic emotion cues for the user turn.

02

Empathetic Reply Planning

A dialogue agent converts perception outputs into an empathy-aware response plan with text, style, avatar, and voice metadata.

03

Affective Speech and Motion

The response is synthesized as expressive speech and converted into lip-synchronized facial motion parameters.

04

3D Avatar Rendering

The motion is merged with avatar-specific assets and rendered as a photorealistic digital human response for browser playback.
